Historic Site of the United State

The National Historic Landmark Madison Historic District is tucked away in limestone bluffs on the banks of the Ohio River in southern Indiana.

Madison's downtown business district is one of the country's largest National Historic Landmark Districts. The more than 133 block historic district is the home of a superb and very large collection of historic buildings. Together they reflect nearly every period of the town's development between 1817 and 1939, ranging from Federal style and Greek Revival mansions to vernacular shotgun houses to institutional and industrial buildings and a vibrant Main Street commercial area lined with two and three story historic buildings. Travel America says, ''Madison is an outdoor museum of architecture.''

Visitors will enter a place that is still a compelling and lively embodiment of pre-World War II small town America. As a hotbed of antislavery activity and an important stop on the Underground Railroad before the Civil War, the historic district also brings alive the story of abolitionism and the flight of slaves from bondage to freedom and those who helped them escape. In Madison, free African Americans established a community with commercial enterprises and independent households.
